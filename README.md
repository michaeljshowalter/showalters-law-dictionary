# Showalter's Law Dictionary

An open-source dictionary of legal terms, defined in the words of the courts that defined them.

## What this is

Each entry is a judicial definition of a legal term: a quotation from a published opinion, accompanied by a full Bluebook citation. The goal is verbatim accuracy, but until a human has pulled the cited opinion and confirmed it, any entry drafted by AI may contain errors in the quote or the cite. The status banner at the top of each entry tells you whether the entry has been cite-checked by a named human or is still provisional.

The dictionary is in the public domain (CC0). Anyone may copy, quote, modify, or republish any part of it without permission.

## How entries are verified

Each entry has a status banner at the top:

- **"This entry has been verified by AI but has not been cite-checked by a human."** The entry was drafted by a large language model from primary sources. The cite is likely accurate, but it has not been independently verified by a human. Do not cite it without verification. If you verify this entry, please update it via pull request—see CONTRIBUTING.md.
- **"This entry was cite-checked by [name] on [date]."** A named human has pulled the cited opinion, confirmed the quoted language appears where the cite says it does, and signed off.

## How to contribute

You can help in two ways:

1. **Cite-check an existing entry.** Pull the cited opinion, verify the quote and pincite are accurate, and open a pull request changing the banner to "cite-checked by [your name] on [date]." Contributors sign their names.
2. **Add a new entry.** Draft an entry for a term not yet included, using a verbatim quote from a published opinion in full Bluebook form. Open a pull request; entries from public-domain judicial opinions are welcome.

See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) for formatting rules and the pull-request checklist.
